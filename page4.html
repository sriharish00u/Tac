⚠️ Know the Risks – Protect Yourself and Others

Understanding risks is the first step toward avoiding them. Every year, thousands of families are destroyed by preventable road accidents. Knowledge is your best defense.

🚨 A Harsh Reality: 85% of road accidents are caused by human error. That means 85% of deaths are preventable. The choice is yours – will you be part of the problem or the solution?
🚀

Over-speeding

The Risk: Reduces reaction time and increases injury severity. At 80 km/h, a crash is like falling from a 7-story building. Speed is a factor in over 60% of fatal accidents.

Prevention: Always follow speed limits. Remember - it's better to arrive late than never.

📱

Distraction

The Risk: Mobile phones increase crash risk by ~4 times. Even 3 seconds of distraction at 60 km/h covers 50 meters. Other distractions include eating, adjusting GPS, and passengers.

Prevention: Keep your phone away. If important, pull over safely to answer.

🍺

Drunk Driving

The Risk: Alcohol impairs judgment, vision, and reaction time. Even one drink can be dangerous. Alcohol affects decision-making and slows reflexes significantly.

Prevention: If you drink, designate a sober driver or book a cab. Never drive after consuming alcohol.

🪖

No Helmet/Seatbelt

The Risk: Dramatically raises fatality risk. Unbelted passengers can become deadly projectiles in crashes. Helmets reduce death risk by 42% in two-wheeler accidents.

Prevention: Always wear helmet/seatbelt before starting the vehicle. Make it a habit.

🏃

Jaywalking

The Risk: Pedestrians crossing unsafely cause many accidents. Jaywalking accounts for a significant percentage of pedestrian fatalities.

Prevention: Always use designated crossings. Wait for traffic to stop before crossing.

😴

Fatigue

The Risk: Drowsy driving is as dangerous as drunk driving. Fatigue impairs reaction time and decision-making. Microsleeps can last 3-5 seconds - enough to cause a crash.

Prevention: Take breaks on long journeys. Get adequate sleep before driving. Stop if feeling drowsy.

🚨 Important: Prevention begins with personal discipline and awareness. Every road death is preventable. Make a commitment to follow traffic rules - your family needs you safe!

🚧 High-Risk Situations – Stay Extra Alert

Certain moments on the road demand extra caution. Being aware of high-risk situations helps you stay prepared and protected.

⏰

Rush Hour Traffic

High volume of vehicles increases collision risk. Be patient, maintain distance, and avoid changing lanes frequently.

🌙

Night Driving

Reduced visibility affects judgment of speed and distance. Use headlights properly and watch for pedestrians and animals.

🛣️

Highways

High speeds mean less time to react. Maintain lane discipline, use indicators, and keep a safe following distance.

🏘️

Residential Areas

Watch for children playing, pets, and parked vehicles. Drive slowly and be ready to stop suddenly.

🎢

Unfamiliar Roads

Reduce speed, follow GPS carefully, watch for unexpected turns or obstacles. Don't rely solely on navigation.

🔧

Poor Vehicle Condition

Worn tires, faulty brakes, or dim lights increase accident risk. Regular maintenance is essential for safety.

👶 Our Children – Protect the Most Vulnerable

Children are our most precious cargo – let's protect them at all costs. Kids don't see danger like adults do. It's our responsibility to keep them safe on roads.

🛡️ Parental Responsibility: Never assume a child will know to stop at a road. Teach them, supervise them, and always take extra precautions around roads.
🧒

Never Leave Children Alone

Never leave children unattended in vehicles. Heat buildup can be fatal within minutes, even with windows cracked.

🪖

Child Helmets

Use appropriate-sized helmets for children on two-wheelers. Adult helmets don't provide proper protection for children.

🧸

Car Seats

Use age-appropriate child car seats. Infants should face backwards. Never hold a child in your lap while driving.

👨‍👩‍👧

Supervision

Always supervise children near roads. Teach them road safety rules from an early age. Be a role model.
