✅ Essential Safety Practices – Make Them Your Habit

Safe driving isn't an option – it's a choice you make every time you get behind the wheel. These practices can mean the difference between coming home safely and causing irreversible tragedy.

💡 Your Actions Save Lives: Following these practices isn't just about protecting yourself – it's about protecting everyone on the road. Your family is waiting for you to come home.
  1. 🪖 Wear ISI-marked helmet – Always wear a certified helmet on two-wheelers. It reduces head injury risk by 72% and saves thousands of lives every year. The helmet strap must be fastened properly.
  2. 🎗️ Use seatbelts – All occupants should wear seatbelts in four-wheelers. The driver must ensure all passengers are buckled up. Reduces fatality risk by 45% in front impacts.
  3. 📱 Avoid mobile phones – Never use mobile while driving, even with hands-free. Even 3 seconds of distraction at 60 km/h covers 50 meters - a deadly distance.
  4. 🍺 Never drink and drive – Even small amounts of alcohol impair judgment, vision, and reaction time. Alcohol affects your ability to judge speed and distance. Book a cab instead.
  5. ⚡ Follow speed limits – 20-30 km/h in school zones, 50 km/h in urban areas, 70 km/h on state highways. Speed limits are calculated based on road conditions and safety requirements.
  6. 🔄 Use indicators – Always check mirrors and use indicators at least 30 meters before changing lanes or turning. Signal your intention early so others can react.
  7. 👀 Maintain visibility – Use headlights in poor visibility, wear reflective clothing at night. Replace dim headlights and broken taillights immediately.
  8. 🛑 Stop at red lights – Never jump signals, even if the road seems empty. Red light running is one of the major causes of intersection crashes.
  9. 🦓 Use zebra crossings – Pedestrians should use designated crossings. Drivers must stop completely at zebra crossings to let pedestrians cross.
  10. 🔧 Maintain your vehicle – Regular checks on brakes, tires, lights, and mirrors. Poor vehicle condition contributes to many accidents.
⚠️ Remember: Safety equipment only works if used correctly. Helmet strap should be fastened snugly under the chin, seatbelt should be snug across your lap and chest - not just across your stomach.

🚗 Defensive Driving – Expect the Unexpected

Defensive driving isn't a skill – it's a mindset that saves lives. The best drivers aren't the fastest; they're the ones who anticipate dangers and react in time.

👁️

Scan the Road

Look 12-15 seconds ahead. Check mirrors every 5-8 seconds. Be aware of vehicles, pedestrians, and road conditions around you.

📏

Maintain Distance

Keep a 3-second gap from the vehicle ahead. Double this in bad weather. This gives you time to react to emergencies.

🅿️

Park Safely

Park in designated areas only. Use parking lights when parked on roads. Always engage parking brake when stopping.

😌

Stay Calm

Don't get provoked by other drivers. Avoid aggressive driving. Patience and courtesy prevent many accidents.

🌧️ Adverse Weather Safety

Weather conditions require extra caution. Here's how to stay safe in different weather conditions.

🌧️

Rainy Conditions

Reduce speed by 30%. Increase following distance. Use headlights even during daytime. Avoid sudden braking. Watch for flooded roads.

🌫️

Foggy Conditions

Use fog lights (not high beams). Drive at reduced speed. Stay in your lane. Listen for approaching vehicles.

☀️

Hot Weather

Check tire pressure regularly. Stay hydrated. Don't leave children or pets in parked vehicles. Watch for melting road surfaces.

🌙

Night Driving

Use low beam headlights when approaching other vehicles. Reduce speed. Watch for pedestrians and animals on roads. Stay extra alert.
